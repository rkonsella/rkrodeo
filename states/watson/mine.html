Best Transportation System
Netherlands

The Netherlands is one of the countries most dedicated to a transportation system consisting of bikes for short trips and trains for long ones. It has two of the top three cities in the Copanhagenize Index , ranking the world's best bicycling cities, and one of the highest ratios of trips taken by train in Europe.

In my experience, I learned that new innovations in the Netherlands are helping perfect this system. All major train stations are being equipped with OV-fiets bike-sharing docks. Now, you can rent bikes in the Netherlands at hundreds of locations with the same card used to ride public transit. I met many young Dutch people who often find themselves in different Dutch cities and keep bicycles parked at the train station of each of those cities, taking up space in increasingly overflowing parking lots. But I also met many people who no longer do this, instead using OV-fiets. Knowing you'll have a bike available to you in any Dutch city with no hassle makes a car-free lifestyle that much easier, and nudges the Netherlands in front of other great transportation networks as the best I've experienced so far.

Ice Rinks Built

When I was in Leh, Ladakh, in northern India, I happened to come across some coaches from The Hockey Foundation playing pond hockey with some local kids on the coolest outdoor rink you've ever seen.

After chatting with them awhile, they found me some equipment to help them coach for a few days. You can see the team I was coaching up at the top of this page. This year, the Hockey Foundation was staging a Guiness World Record attempt for the highest elevation hockey game at a site over 14,000ft near the border with China in Ladakh. They needed a regulation rink, including regulation boards, and were short for time after the boards were held up for months with the Indian government. This led to an all hands on deck situation where I spent a few days at the site helping set up boards and paint rink lines. The world record event went smoothly, and I was even able to play in one of the games! It was an unbelievable time for me, and it all came to be when I happened to be in the right place at the right time.

Chibra Kargyam, Ladakh, India

Favorite Piece of Infrastructure
Hovenring
Eindhoven, Netherlands

The Hovenring is a suspended bicycle roundabout in Eindhoven, Netherlands, the only structure of it's kind in the world. It was one of just a few pieces of infrastructure that, prior to my Watson, I had decided to eventually visit.

After riding around it myself, I was able to get in touch with Irmo Kaal, the project manager for the city of Eindhoven. Mr. Kaal conceived and captained the construction of the bridge, and was able to provide all kinds of information on the costs, issues, and design process of the project. He gave me a presentation on the process of building it, teaching me the Dutch mindset that enables a project like this to happen in the Netherlands.
