Besseggen, Norway

Alright… Sweden. I was in Gothenburg for almost five days (plus a one-day preview of Malmo taking a day trip from Copenhagen. Not gonna lie, the entire purpose was to go to a music festival called Way Out West. I saw the lineup, I’ve been trying to see Frank Ocean for years and thought, “eh, why not?”

What I Did

I got to see three days of music in a beautiful park called Slottsskogen; three main stages and two smaller stages took up a portion of the park for the week. The festival was vegetertain, which I was surprised about, but I spent three days eating fried halloumi burgers, which was fine by me.

Before and after the festival I spent some time exploring Gothenburg, including finding some really legit tacos at a restaurant opened by a Mexican woman from San Francisco (I miss Mexican food). Gothenburg was nice and hilly, lots of cool restaurants and bars, it would be a cool place to spend more than just a few days.

Who I Saw

Of course, the point of the festival was to see music. Luckily, some favorites were there.

Frank Ocean

The biggest reason to go. I’m a huge Frank Ocean fan (go listen to Channel Orange), and he doesn’t tour much so I hadn’t found a chance to see him before. He was great.

The xx

Another one I really like but hadn’t seen before. The xx was one of my favorite bands when their first album came out in 2009, so this was long overdue.

Jens Lekman

Alright I hadn’t heard of him until I was told to listen to him like a week before the festival and he quickly became a favorite. No idea why I hadn’t listened to him before. Not only was he great, but he made it a point to have an all-female backing band, something I hadn’t seen before.

Others

Lots of other acts, too. Some I’m a fan of, others not so much. Let’s just throw them in two tiers, artists I actually like and those I don’t.

I liked seeing The Shins, Danny Brown, Vince Staples, Mac DeMarco, Feist, Band of Horses, and Regina Spektor.

I didn't really care about seeing Migos, Major Lazer, or Lana del Rey.
